# Joseph Carlson After Hours - November 10, 2025
## Module 2: Episode Index & Glossary

### 2.1. Primary Subjects & Themes

- Why today is the best time in human history to be an investor (structural advantages, zero-fee trading, abundant education, multiple innovation waves)

- The systematic negativity bias in social media algorithms and its impact on investor psychology

- Visa/Mastercard merchant settlement and its actual impact on payment processors vs. banks

- Proposed $2,000 stimulus payments and the wealth transfer mechanism to shareholders

- Portfolio review and performance analysis (Carlson's $1.3M portfolio, 6-year track record)

- Investment cycle selloffs as buying opportunities (Meta, DoorDash, Duolingo, Uber, Pinterest)

- Apple valuation analysis and skepticism of Gene Monster's bullish call

- Meta as an attractive buy below $600 despite capex concerns

- The superiority of public equity markets over real estate and other asset classes

- Professional workplace conduct and effective career advancement strategies

### 2.2. Glossary of People, Places & Passing Mentions

**Gene Monster:** Analyst who appeared on CNBC predicting Apple would be the top-performing Mag 7 stock over the next 3-6 months, citing the upcoming AI-powered Siri release as a catalyst for 20% upside.

**Dan Ives:** Wedbush analyst who appeared on CNBC calling Meta a "table pounder" buy opportunity below $600, arguing that Mark Zuckerberg's AI infrastructure spending will drive massive earnings growth.

**Mark Mahaney:** Analyst discussing why investors dislike investment cycles and why companies like DoorDash, Duolingo, and Uber represent buying opportunities after announcing increased spending.

**Mark Zuckerberg:** CEO of Meta, described by Dan Ives as operating as a "wartime CEO" in the context of the AI arms race.

**Sundar Pichai:** Mentioned as CEO of Google, one of the "great founders" investors can align with.

**Carlson's Father:** Referenced as representative of the baby boomer generation that faced $150 trading commissions, 2% mutual fund fees, and no accessible stock market education.

**Wall Street Journal:** News source for the Visa/Mastercard merchant settlement story.

**CNBC:** Financial news network where analyst interviews (Gene Monster, Dan Ives, Mark Mahaney) were broadcast.

**Qualium.com:** Carlson's self-built stock analysis platform, promoted throughout the episode as offering tools like DCF calculators, earnings summaries, and KPIs for $10/month.

**Trump Administration:** Referenced in context of potentially implementing $2,000 stimulus payments and tangentially in the workplace confrontation video.

**Visa, Mastercard, American Express:** Payment processing companies that settled with merchants to lower interchange fees by 0.1% on average.

**Amazon, Netflix, Google, Microsoft, S&P Global:** Major holdings in Carlson's portfolio, referenced as examples of high-quality compounding machines.

**Duolingo:** Language learning app and Carlson portfolio holding that is currently underperforming after prioritizing content quality over short-term conversions.

**DoorDash:** Food delivery company whose stock sold off after announcing autonomous vehicle investments and acquiring Deliveroo.

**Uber:** Ride-sharing company mentioned as one of several experiencing investment cycle selloffs.

**Pinterest:** Social media platform briefly mentioned as another company facing investor concerns about increased spending.

**Deliveroo:** British food delivery company acquired by DoorDash, mentioned as requiring investment to regrow.

**Airbnb:** Travel platform cited by Mark Mahaney as showing strong demand despite consumer weakness narratives.

**COVID-19 Pandemic:** Referenced as historical precedent for how stimulus checks flow into corporate earnings and stock market investments.

**Boomer Generation:** Referenced as the demographic that had affordable housing but faced prohibitive stock trading costs and limited investment education.

**Government Shutdown:** Mentioned briefly as a concern being resolved, contributing to market gains on the day of recording.

**Union Employees/HR Director:** Unnamed company and individuals featured in the "fail of the week" segment about workplace confrontation over layoffs.

**Netflix, YouTube TV, YouTube Premium:** Streaming services mentioned as examples of what consumers might purchase with stimulus money.

**Instagram Reels, TikTok, Twitter:** Social media platforms referenced in discussion of algorithmic negativity bias.

**S&P 500:** Broad market index recommended as the ETF foundation for 50% of portfolio allocation.

**Mag 7:** Informal term for the seven largest technology companies (Apple, Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta, Tesla, Nvidia), referenced in context of stock performance predictions.

**Google Cloud, Azure, AWS:** Cloud computing platforms mentioned as comparison points for Meta's AI infrastructure spending (more predictable than Meta's investments).

**Siri:** Apple's voice assistant, with new AI-powered version expected March-April 2025 serving as Gene Monster's bullish catalyst.
