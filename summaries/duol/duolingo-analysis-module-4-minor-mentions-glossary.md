## Minor Strategies/Concepts

### Tax Loss Harvesting
**Context:** Raised as a potential strategy for dealing with Duolingo's losses, but not pursued.

**Description:** Selling losing positions to realize capital losses for tax purposes, then potentially repurchasing after wash-sale rule period (30 days).

**Carlson's Approach:**
> "Do we sell out of the position? Do we accept our losses, do some tax loss harvesting, and move on?"

**Decision:** Carlson chose **not** to tax-loss harvest, instead holding the position based on improving fundamentals.

**Implication:** Tax benefits should not override investment thesis. If fundamentals support holding, tax harvesting is secondary consideration.

---

### Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)
**Context:** Mentioned as potential strategy for adding to Duolingo position.

**Description:** Incrementally purchasing shares over time at different prices to average down cost basis during volatile periods.

**Carlson's Plan:**
> "As long as I see that happen [metrics improving], I'm not going to sell the stock. And in fact, I may dollar cost average and buy a bit more."

**Application:** DCA is appropriate when:
- High conviction in long-term thesis
- Near-term volatility expected to continue
- Want to avoid trying to "time the bottom"
- Spreading capital deployment reduces emotional pressure

### Rerating / Multiple Compression
**Context:** Central concept throughout stream explaining Duolingo's stock decline.

**Description:** When market changes the valuation multiple (P/E, P/S, EV/Sales) it assigns to a stock based on revised growth expectations, competitive threats, or risk assessment. Typically occurs when growth stocks miss guidance or show deceleration.

**Key Mechanics:**
> "Anytime a highly rated stock, one that's trading at a higher multiple, has any concerns with its growth, investors sell out of the stock. They rate it much lower."

**Example:** If a stock trades at 15x forward sales assuming 40% growth, and growth slows to 25%, the multiple might compress to 8-10x sales, causing a 30-50% stock decline even if the business remains healthy.

### Bookings (SaaS Metric)
**Context:** The specific metric that triggered Duolingo's rerating.

**Description:** Forward-looking revenue committed by customers, typically through subscriptions or contracts. For subscription businesses, bookings precede revenue recognition by the subscription period (often 12 months).

**Relationship to Revenue:**
> "The way to think of this is right now the revenue that Dualingo is receiving this quarter is bookings they got last year. Meaning that the bookings they get this year is going to be the revenue they receive next year."

**Why It Matters:** Bookings growth is a **leading indicator** of future revenue. Slowing bookings signal revenue deceleration 1-4 quarters in the future.

**Duolingo's Issue:** Q4 bookings guidance of 22% YoY vs. 24% Wall Street expectation = future revenue will decelerate more than anticipated.

### Voting Machine vs. Weighing Machine (Benjamin Graham)
**Context:** Used to explain short-term vs. long-term stock price dynamics.

**Description:** Graham's metaphor distinguishing short-term sentiment-driven price movements from long-term fundamental value realization.

**The Quote:**
> "Benjamin Graham the great investor is famous for saying in the short term the stock market is a voting machine in the long term it's a weighing machine."

**Application:**
- **Short-term (voting):** Price reflects popularity, momentum, sentiment
- **Long-term (weighing):** Price reflects actual business value, earnings power, competitive position

**Implication for Investors:**
Focus on building/identifying "heavy companies" (strong fundamentals) rather than reacting to short-term "votes" (price movements).

### Engagement Metrics (DAU/MAU)
**Context:** Critical metrics used to evaluate Duolingo's fundamental health.

**Definitions:**
- **DAU (Daily Active Users):** Users who engage with the product every day
- **MAU (Monthly Active Users):** Users who engage at least once per month
- **DAU/MAU Ratio:** Engagement intensity (higher ratio = more habitual usage)

**Duolingo's Metrics:**
- **DAU:** 50.5M (up from 47.7M QoQ)
- **MAU:** 135M (+20% YoY)
- **DAU/MAU Ratio:** ~37% (high for educational app)

**Why They Matter:**
> "This level of engagement having 50 plus million daily active users surpasses many top social media companies."

High engagement indicates:
- Strong product-market fit
- Habit formation success
- Defensible moat (switching costs from streak loss, progress investment)
- Future monetization potential

### Network Effects & Data Moats
**Context:** Implicit in discussion of platform companies and Duolingo's competitive position.

**Description:** Business advantages that strengthen with scale:
- **Network effects:** Product becomes more valuable as more users join (e.g., language partner matching, competitive leaderboards)
- **Data moats:** More users → more usage data → better AI/personalization → better product → more users (virtuous cycle)

**Application to Duolingo:**
- 50M DAUs generate billions of learning interactions
- Data trains AI models for personalization, content difficulty optimization, retention strategies
- Scale enables content investment (Chess course, video call features)
