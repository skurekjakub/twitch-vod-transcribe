# Duolingo Q3 2025 Earnings Call - Qualitative & Sentiment Analysis

**Company:** Duolingo, Inc. (DUOL)  
**Period:** Q3 2025 (Quarter Ended September 30, 2025)  
**Call Participants:** CEO Luis von Ahn, CFO Matt Skaruppa

## Management Tone: Confident but Defensive

### Overall Sentiment: **7/10 (Cautiously Optimistic)**

**Confident On:**
- Long-term AI opportunity ("fundamental transformation of education")
- Operating leverage and margin expansion (29% EBITDA margins achieved)
- Product roadmap execution (guided calls, chess, math expansion)
- User growth trajectory (stabilizing at healthy 30% YoY)
- China growth potential (fastest-growing country, 5-6% of business)

**Defensive/Cautious On:**
- Near-term bookings deceleration (spent significant time justifying Q4 slowdown)
- Max underperformance ("underperforming our lofty expectations")
- Strategic shift timing ("over the last month or two" suggests reactive, not planned)
- Lack of specificity on when monetization re-accelerates

**Evasive On:**
- Duolingo English Test revenue decline (-10% YoY, no commentary)
- Exact magnitude of Q4 bookings impact from strategic shift
- 2026 financial guidance (avoided providing any numbers)
- Max pricing strategy and penetration targets

## Key Themes from Prepared Remarks

### 1. **AI as Transformational Opportunity (Dominant Theme)**

**Opening Statement (CEO von Ahn):**
> "We had a strong Q3 with solid performance across all metrics. And we are on track for another exceptional year. More than 50 million people now use Duolingo every day... And yet, we still feel **early in our journey**. We believe **AI will fundamentally transform education**, and we have **line of sight to building a product that teaches better than ever before.** That's what makes this such an exciting moment for Duolingo."

**Frequency:** AI mentioned 18 times during the call (excluding analyst questions)

**Tone:** Evangelical, visionary, borderline hyperbolic
- "Teaches better than ever before"
- "Fundamental transformation"
- "Billions of users" (vs. 135M MAUs today)
- "As good as a human tutor, but that is also more engaging"

**Strategic Framing:** Management positioned the strategic shift as an **offensive move** (capitalizing on opportunity) rather than a defensive one (responding to weakness). This is critical for maintaining investor confidence.

**Red Flag Language:** 
- "Early in our journey" (despite being a public company since 2021, suggesting long road ahead)
- "Line of sight" (not yet achieved, just visible on horizon)

---

### 2. **Strategic Pivot Justification (Secondary Theme)**

**Framing as "Balancing Act" (CFO Skaruppa):**
> "We've talked to you all and our investors for a long time about wanting to **maximize platform LTV**. And what Luis is describing is a change of, you know, **small proportion right now** that is going to help us grow users for a long time, get users to do more lessons, learn better, spend more time on the app, and in general, be more engaged Duolingo users."

**Key Language Choices:**
1. **"Small proportion"** â€“ Minimizing the magnitude of the shift
2. **"Balancing act"** â€“ Suggests this is normal course of business, not a radical pivot
3. **"We've always done"** â€“ Implies continuity, not change

**Reality Check:** Management introduced this as a new priority shift "over the last month or two," which contradicts the "we've always done this" framing. This tension suggests **management is nervous about investor reaction** to bookings deceleration.

### 3. **Product Velocity & Execution Confidence (Tertiary Theme)**

**2026 Product Roadmap:**

## Red Flags & Concerning Language

### ðŸš© Red Flag #1: "One-Time" Tax Benefit Dominates Reported Results

**Language Tone:** Transparent but repetitive (suggests concern that investors won't understand the non-recurring nature)

### ðŸš© Red Flag #2: Strategic Shift Timing ("Last Month or Two")

**CEO von Ahn:**
> "What has happened is that **over the last month or two**, I've really rallied the company towards this shift."

**Why This Is Concerning:**
1. **Short timeframe** suggests reactive decision (not long-term strategic planning)
2. **"Rallied the company"** implies internal resistance or need to convince team
3. **Q4 guidance cut** announced in same breath as strategic shift (correlation = causation?)

**Alternative Interpretation:** Management saw Q4 bookings tracking weak, so retroactively framed it as a strategic choice (rather than admitting weakness).

**Benefit of the Doubt:** CEO genuinely believes in AI opportunity and made bold decision to sacrifice near-term for long-term. Timing is coincidental.

### ðŸš© Red Flag #3: "Underperforming Our Lofty Expectations" (Max Subscribers)

**CFO Skaruppa:**
> "Max is now 9% of our subscribers. It doubled in Q3 year over year in terms of bookings. So you know, it's clearly doing well in that regard. It's **underperforming our lofty expectations** for it, though."

**Why This Matters:**
- **"Lofty"** is defensive language (lowers bar for future performance)
- **9% penetration** after 2+ years of Max availability is slow adoption
- **Doubling YoY** sounds good, but off a small base (9% of 11.5M = ~1M Max subs)

**Management Response:** Guided video calls will unlock Max for beginners (largest cohort). But this is an admission that **current Max offering is too advanced for most users**.

**Fact:** DET revenue declined **-10% YoY** in Q3 2025 (from $10.8M to $9.6M)

**Possible Explanations:**
