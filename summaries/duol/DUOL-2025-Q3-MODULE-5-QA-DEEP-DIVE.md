# Duolingo Q3 2025 Earnings Call - Q&A Session Deep Dive

**Company:** Duolingo, Inc. (DUOL)  
**Period:** Q3 2025 (Quarter Ended September 30, 2025)  
**Total Questions:** 12 from 11 analysts

## Main Analyst Concerns (Ranked by Frequency)

### 1. **Strategic Shift Rationale & Timeline (5 questions)**
**Analysts:** Nathan Feather (Morgan Stanley), Ralph Schackart (William Blair), Wyatt Swanson (D.A. Davidson), Ryan MacDonald (Needham), Andrew Boone (Citizens)

**Core Concern:** Why is management pivoting to long-term user growth *now*, and how long will bookings pressure last?

**Representative Question (Nathan Feather):**
> "Given the scale of the opportunity ahead that you're talking to, is the focus on greater, you know, long-term prioritization a **durable shift in strategy** that we should expect to continue, or would you plan to return to the current prioritization mix at some point? And then in connection with that focus, should we expect the pressure on bookings that you're seeing in Q4 to **continue into next year** with the durability of that shift?"

**Management Response Quality: 5/10 (Defensive, Vague)**

**CEO von Ahn:**
- "Well okay. So it's gonna be **years until we get to a point** where we have enough that I think is, you know, just the best possible way to learn"
- "Over the **last month or two**, I've really rallied the company towards this shift"
- Avoided committing to specific timeline for bookings recovery

**CFO Skaruppa:**
- "It's a **relatively small, you know, financial impact** from this kind of reprioritization"
- "We're down to bookings and the financials ultimately as well. I think, you know, there's a we guided to a really strong 2025"
- **Key admission:** "Would we expect some of that to persist into 2026? **Sure.**"

**What Analysts Wanted but Didn't Get:**
1. Quantification of bookings impact (e.g., "strategic shift will cost us 3-5 points of growth in 2026")
2. Specific milestones for when the shift "pays off" (e.g., "we expect DAU growth to re-accelerate to 35%+ by H2 2026")
3. Explanation of why "the last month or two" is the right time (vs. suspicion it's reactive to weak Q4 bookings)

**Verdict:** Management was **evasive** on the most important investor question (near-term financial impact), choosing instead to focus on long-term vision. This creates a **modeling black hole** for 2026.

### 2. **Bookings Growth Deceleration (4 questions)**
**Analysts:** Bryan Smilek (JPMorgan), Mark Mahaney (Evercore ISI), Justin Patterson (KeyBanc), Andrew Boone (Citizens)

**Core Concern:** Why are bookings decelerating from +33% (Q3) to +24% (Q4 implied), and is it due to strategic shift or underlying weakness?

**Representative Question (Andrew Boone):**
> "Can you just give us a sense of, you know, the duration of this, pivot or shift? Is this something that's gonna take I don't know, all through 2026? Is it more short term in nature?"

**Management Response Quality: 6/10 (Transparent on Drivers, Vague on Duration)**

**CEO von Ahn:**
- **Specific example provided:** "If right now, free users get 25 energy units at the beginning of the day... If we were to do an experiment that decreases that from 25 to, say, 24... we know that would make us more money... However, we also know that would decrease daily active users... we would not launch that experiment going from 25 to 24 even if it meant, you know, quite a bit of bookings gains, if it has a real hit on daily active users."
- This was the **clearest explanation** of what the strategic shift means in practice

**CFO Skaruppa:**
- "In September, we saw some of this [strategic shift impact], and what it looked like was **slightly lower free to pay conversion**. That free to pay conversion was **still growing year over year**. So it was still good free to pay conversion. It was just on the margin. It was slightly lower."
- **Key insight:** Conversion is still positive YoY, just decelerating (not collapsing)

**What Analysts Wanted but Didn't Get:**
1. Quantified impact on Q4 bookings (e.g., "strategic shift accounts for 3-4 points of the 9-point deceleration")
2. Baseline bookings growth rate absent the strategic shift (e.g., "we'd be at +28-30% without the pivot")
3. Confidence interval on Q4 bookings (New Year's promo variability mentioned but not quantified)

**Verdict:** Management provided **good color on mechanics** (energy example) but avoided **quantifying magnitude**. This suggests either:
- Uncertainty (they don't know the exact impact yet)
- Strategic ambiguity (don't want to be held to a number)

### 3. **Duolingo Max Performance & Adoption (3 questions)**
**Analysts:** Alex Sklar (Raymond James), Andrew Boone (Citizens), Shweta Khajuria (Wolfe)

**Core Concern:** Max is "underperforming lofty expectations" despite doubling YoY ‚Äì what's the path to re-acceleration?

**Representative Question (Alex Sklar):**
> "On the last call, you brought up this idea on video call about **average number of words spoken per session**. And you know, that was kind of a new metric you were going to testing towards. What have you learned so far now that you've kind of been optimizing for that metric?"

**Management Response Quality: 8/10 (Specific, Data-Driven)**

**CEO von Ahn:**
- "We've managed to move it. This is important to know about our company. Whenever we **fixate on a metric**, we are very good at moving it."
- "**More than doubled this year** in terms of average number of words spoken per Max subscriber"
- **Key product insight:** "Video call at the moment is a **monolingual experience** in the language that you're learning... That's great for practicing Spanish, but for beginner users, it's **too hard**."
- **Solution:** "Guided video calls. Which are basically **bilingual**. So it's if you're an English speaker learning Spanish, this would be **part in English, part in Spanish**, and it's a lot easier for beginner users."
- **Proof point:** "When we give that to beginner users, they actually **speak more words per call** because they're actually able to do something."

**CFO Skaruppa:**
- "Max right now, again, it's they're still small as they've been ramping up, but was renewing **slightly better than Super**."
- **Key caveat:** "But it's early, and Q4 is a relatively cohort. Relatively sizable cohort. **So we'll talk about this again on the next call.**"

**What Analysts Wanted but Didn't Get:**
1. Max pricing (never disclosed)
2. Max unit economics (LTV:CAC, gross margin vs. Super)
3. Target Max penetration rate (e.g., "we think Max can reach 15-20% of subs by 2026")
4. Competitive benchmarking (e.g., how does Max compare to Babbel Live, Rosetta Stone premium tiers)

**Verdict:** Management provided **excellent product detail** on video calls (2x engagement improvement, bilingual solution), but avoided **financial specifics** on Max economics. This suggests Max is **strategically important but not yet a financial needle-mover**.

### 4. **DAU Growth Sustainability (3 questions)**
**Analysts:** Bryan Smilek (JPMorgan), Nathan Feather (Morgan Stanley), Ryan MacDonald (Needham)

**Core Concern:** Can 30% YoY DAU growth be sustained, or will it decelerate further?

**Representative Question (Bryan Smilek):**
> "Can you help us better understand the underlying drivers of DAU growth into 4Q engagement overall? And how close do you think you are to getting back on track in terms of **marketing in the US**? I know you mentioned growing impression volume. But is that starting to translate to user growth now?"

**Management Response Quality: 7/10 (Transparent, Specific)**

**CEO von Ahn:**
- **Q3 drivers (detailed):**
  1. **Positive:** Luckin partnership in Asia (+users), product improvements (+retention)
  2. **Negative:** Paused "unhinged posts" on social media (hurt viral growth)
- **Recovery:** "Over the last few weeks, we have started the unhinged posts again in our social media accounts, and while it hasn't gotten all the way to the peak where it was, we've seen **a lot of recovery**."
- **Q4 guidance:** "We expect **some amount of deceleration** from Q3. But we are pretty happy with where it has stabilized. **September and October were both at around 30% year-over-year growth** for DAUs. And that's comping a pretty strong quarter last year."

**CFO Skaruppa:**
- "There is a you know, we talked about it on the last call, which is we've had some success particularly in the US **spending a little bit more on actual marketing**, seeing a nice return in the US in particular."
- **Scale:** "It's relatively small dollars, but we're gonna continue that into Q4"

**What Analysts Wanted but Didn't Get:**
1. US vs. International DAU growth breakdown (e.g., "US growing at +15%, International at +40%")
2. Quantified impact of Luckin partnership (e.g., "added 2M DAUs in China")
3. Social media ROI (e.g., "viral posts drive X% of new user acquisition at $Y CAC")
4. Churn rate trends (are you retaining users better, or just acquiring more?)

**Verdict:** Management provided **good qualitative color** on DAU drivers but avoided **quantifying contributions**. The 30% YoY stabilization guidance for Q4 is **credible and healthy** (not a red flag).

### 5. **Product Roadmap Execution (3 questions)**
**Analysts:** Wyatt Swanson (D.A. Davidson), Ralph Schackart (William Blair), Miguel Arounian (Citi)

**Core Concern:** Can Duolingo execute on ambitious 2026 product roadmap (guided calls, chess PVP, math expansion, etc.)?

**Representative Question (Miguel Arounian):**
> "Does that accelerate your road map in terms of adding new language learning modules? I know within the ones that you currently have, but **moving it to new subjects**. And, you know, what is it about that what's changing right now around AI that's letting you do that, today?"

**Management Response Quality: 9/10 (Specific, Credible)**

**CEO von Ahn:**
- **Language expansion:** "You'll see us go faster in terms of adding content to these **top nine languages**. And right now, for most of them, we don't get you to the place where we wanna get you, is the, a **Duolingo score of 130**. Which is equivalent to CFR level of B2, which is where you can get a knowledge job in that language. You will see that over the **next few months** we're going to be adding content that can do that for all the top nine languages."
- **New subjects:** "At the moment, we're not working on any other subjects. I'm not gonna say that we're not gonna add other subjects next year. That may be the case. You know, like you saw with chess, it took us **nine months from idea to actually launching**."
- **Chess proof point:** "It is the **fastest growing course** that we had. It's growing much faster than math and music... It's true. We have millions of users... it's already surpassed math and music. The retention of chess users... over the last three to four months is **slightly higher than language learning**."

**What Analysts Wanted but Didn't Get:**
1. R&D budget allocation by product area (e.g., "60% on languages, 20% on chess, 10% on math, 10% on music")
2. DAU targets by course (e.g., "we expect chess to reach 5M DAUs by end of 2026")
3. Monetization strategy for non-language courses (e.g., "chess will have separate subscription tier")

**Verdict:** Management provided **strong execution evidence** (chess is working, timelines are specific). This is the **most credible part of the bull case** ‚Äì Duolingo can ship products that work.

### 6. **Competitive Threats & AI Disruption (2 questions)**
**Analysts:** Miguel Arounian (Citi), Justin Patterson (KeyBanc)

**Core Concern:** Is generative AI (ChatGPT, real-time translation) an existential threat to Duolingo's business model?

**Representative Question (Miguel Arounian):**
> "How is that [AI] impacting the competitive landscape... why would anybody wanna learn a language with Duolingo when you can just learn it with ChatGPT?"

**Management Response Quality: 9/10 (Confident, Data-Backed)**

**CEO von Ahn:**
- **Engagement moat:** "We're **not particularly worried** about that. You know, the main thing that we do really well not only do we teach well, the main thing that we do really well is **keep people engaged**. And in order to learn a language, you need to be engaged for years... With ChatGPT, you can go there and you can ask it to teach you a few words here and there. But, you know, it's not like you can have really **curriculum for years**."
- **Translation threat:** "I believe in 100% of the Google IO conferences over the last ten years, they have showcased simultaneous language translation. They do it every single year, and it's good. It works. But this has been happening for the last ten years and **we have not seen the desire to learn a language go down at all** in fact, it has come up."
- **User cohort defense:**
  1. **Hobby learners:** "It kinda doesn't matter whether a computer can do that. It's because they're the same with chess, by the way. **Computers are way better than humans at chess**, but still, you know, we have millions of people wanting to learn chess."
  2. **Job-seeking English learners:** "People who are learning English and they actually wanna learn English... being able to have, a phone that they have to hold out, it just kinda that's not what they wanna do."

**What Analysts Wanted but Didn't Get:**
1. Competitive win/loss data (e.g., "we surveyed churned users, and <5% cited AI tools as reason for leaving")
2. Feature parity timeline (e.g., "ChatGPT can do X, we can do Y, here's our roadmap to match/exceed")
3. Partnership opportunities (e.g., "we're exploring integration with ChatGPT for conversational practice")

**Verdict:** Management's **dismissive confidence** is backed by data (translation available for 10 years, demand still growing). This is the **strongest bull case argument** ‚Äì Duolingo's moat is engagement, not technology.

### 7. **Pricing Strategy & ARPU Growth (2 questions)**
**Analysts:** Mark Mahaney (Evercore ISI), Andrew Boone (Citizens)

**Core Concern:** How will pricing evolve, and can ARPU growth continue at mid-single-digit pace?

**Representative Question (Mark Mahaney):**
> "I know you had some price actions or price increases earlier in the year. What kind of reactions have you seen to those? Secondly, I think you were gonna hold off on doing any other I think it was just on, like, new people coming in on the standard plan. But **your thoughts on rolling out other price increases**."

**Management Response Quality: 8/10 (Transparent, Data-Driven)**

**CEO von Ahn:**
- "In terms of price increases, you'll see us we'll be testing prices. We'll be testing all kinds of things... But you'll see us **test prices. And by that, I mean, up and down**. We'll probably test some prices much lower or a package... We'll probably do a **package that is, like, half price, that is, like, you know, super light**."
- **Framing:** "We'll see us launch the things that we think are good for the whole platform. I don't know exactly what's going to win."

**CFO Skaruppa:**
- "**ARPU has gone up this year every quarter. Kinda mid-single digits.** That's also reflected in the guide. That's **mainly come more from Max than it has from price point changes**."
- **New Year's promo:** "We did take pricing at various times over the past little bit. And that does influence our ability to **discount during our one and only discount of the year**... One of the things that happens every Q4 when we talk about it is we talk about the ability in our Q4 bookings guide because Q4 is our most variable quarter because we run this **New Year's promotion**."

**What Analysts Wanted but Didn't Get:**
1. Price elasticity data (e.g., "10% price increase leads to 3% churn increase, net positive")
2. Super Light package pricing (e.g., "$4.99/month vs. $9.99 for Super")
3. Max pricing and planned increases (e.g., "Max is $19.99/month, considering $24.99 test")
4. Geographic pricing variations (e.g., "India pricing is 1/3 of US pricing")

**Verdict:** Management is **testing thoughtfully** (up and down pricing) rather than just extracting revenue. The willingness to test **lower-priced packages** is refreshing and suggests **long-term thinking** (expand TAM vs. maximize ARPU).
