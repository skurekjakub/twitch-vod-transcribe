## $DUOL (Duolingo) - Primary Focus

### Main Thesis

**Contrarian accumulation opportunity in a misunderstood strategic pivot.** The market has violently repriced Duolingo due to near-term bookings deceleration, but this deceleration is an intentional strategic choice by management to capture a once-in-a-generation opportunity in AI-powered education. The company is sacrificing 12-24 months of revenue optimization to position for "billions of users" rather than "hundreds of millions." Current holder; may add on further weakness if fundamentals continue improving.

**Strategy**: Long-term hold with potential dollar-cost averaging if user metrics continue growing while stock remains depressed.

---

### Supporting Evidence: The "Why"

#### Technical Evidence

**The Rerating Magnitude:**
> "The stock price is being obliterated... It's down a staggering 29% on the day."

**Chart Pattern Context:**
The stock has now fallen approximately **50% from recent highs**, moving from a 2% portfolio position to less than 1% position due to price decline alone.

**Volume & Sentiment:**
While not explicitly discussed with technical indicators, the single-day 29% decline on earnings represents **capitulation-level selling** typically associated with forced liquidation (stop-loss triggers, algorithmic selling, institutional mandate violations). This type of violent repricing often creates short-term oversold conditions.

#### Fundamental Evidence

**The Trigger (What Wall Street Sees):**

> "When we look at the 2025 outlook, they have something here called bookings. The Q4 bookings, so next quarter to grow 22% year-over-year or 19% on a constant currency basis at the midpoint. Now, why is this important? It's important because Wall Street believed that they were going to grow their bookings at around 24%."

**The Miss:** 22% guidance vs. 24% expectation = **2 percentage point deceleration**

**What Bookings Mean:**
> "The way to think of this is right now the revenue that Dualingo is receiving this quarter is bookings they got last year. Meaning that the bookings they get this year is going to be the revenue they receive next year. So bookings are a preview of the future and the preview of the future shows revenue slowing down."

**Critical Insight:** Bookings are a **leading indicator**. This 22% number telegraphs that 2025 revenue growth will decelerate from the current 40%+ levels to low-20% range.

**The Positive Fundamentals (What the Market Is Ignoring):**

**1. Subscription Revenue Growth:**
> "If we look at the overall subscription revenue of the company, it is growing incredibly fast. In fact, the subscription revenue grew by 45%. Subscription revenue makes up around 90% of their total revenue because they are a subscription-based company."

**Interpretation:** The core business model is accelerating (45% growth), not decelerating. The bookings slowdown is forward-looking guidance, not current reality.

**2. Monthly Active Users (MAU) - Refutation of Q2 Concern:**
> "A big problem with Dualingo a quarter ago was that their monthly active users actually declined. It declined. Oh no. Everybody was concerned about the monthly active users going down. Will it ever grow again? Was this because AI is eating away at Dualingo's mode? Was this because Google Translate is now stealing customers? Well, this quarter proved that wrong. It continued to grow even past where it was in Q1. The growth of the monthly active users is up 20% year-over-year, and now they have 135 million monthly active users."

**Key Data Points:**
- **135M MAU** (up 20% YoY)
- **Reversed Q2 decline** (critical psychological barrier overcome)
- **Refutes competitive threat thesis** (AI/translation tools not stealing users)

**3. Daily Active Users (DAU) - Engagement Strength:**
> "These are people that log on and do a lesson every single day, every day of the month, the entire quarter. We have it right here. It's now past 50 million daily active users. We look at this and it went from 47.7 to 50.5. Now this level of engagement having 50 plus million daily active users surpasses many top social media companies."

**Key Data Points:**
- **50.5M DAU** (up from 47.7M QoQ = +5.9% sequential growth)
- **Engagement level competitive with major social platforms**
- **Crosses psychological 50M milestone**

**4. Paid Subscribers:**
> "The paid subscribers also grew by 34% year-over-year. Now they have 11.5 million. That's a bump up from 10.9 million the quarter before."

**Key Data Points:**
- **11.5M paid subscribers** (+34% YoY)
- **10.9M → 11.5M QoQ** = +5.5% sequential growth
- **Conversion still working despite less monetization focus**

**Summary of Fundamental Evidence:**
| Metric | Q3 2024 | Growth | Interpretation |
|--------|---------|--------|----------------|
| Subscription Revenue | - | +45% YoY | Core business accelerating |
| MAU | 135M | +20% YoY | User base expanding, Q2 fears disproven |

**The Disconnect:** Every *current* metric is strong or accelerating. The only weak metric is *forward guidance*, which management explains is **intentional**.

#### Macro/Trend Context: The Strategic Pivot

**CEO Luis von Ahn's Thesis:**

> "There's there's a huge opportunity right now. We see a huge opportunity um over the next few years. education and the way people learn um are they're going to change fundamentally and and it's because of AI... We would be talking about billions of users that we have and that's what we want to shoot for here. So this is why we are we are um investing in the long term."

**The Multi-Year Vision:**
1. **Current state**: 135M MAU, mostly language learning
2. **Opportunity**: AI enables human-tutor-quality teaching at scale
3. **Goal**: Billions of users across expanded educational content
4. **Time horizon**: "Next few years"
5. **Trade-off**: Near-term monetization for long-term user capture

**The Intentionality Evidence:**

Von Ahn provides a specific example of a monetization lever they're choosing NOT to pull:

> "If right now um uh a free user free users get 25 energy units at the beginning of the day uh and every exercise that they do spends one unit. If we were to do an experiment that decreases that from 25 to say 24, that's one fewer unit of of of energy per day. We know that would make us more money... However, we also know that would decrease daily active users because it would frustrate some of the users."

> "In the fictitious experiment that I just uh gave you, we would not launch that experiment going from 25 to 24 energy units, even if it meant, you know, quite a bit of bookings gains if it has uh a real hit on daily active users."

**Carlson's Interpretation:**
> "Dualingo has these hundreds of experiments they're running all the time, testing their product on learning outcomes and behaviors and engagement, their user retention and churn, all these different things that they're constantly tuning and turning. And what they're saying is with all of these tests, they're now focusing primarily on user growth and user retention. They're not focusing quite as much on conversion or revenue."

**Connection to Market-Wide "Platform Evolution" Theme:**

This fits the Netflix/Amazon/Uber playbook:
1. **Phase 1**: Build user base, optimize engagement (current Duolingo phase)
2. **Phase 2**: Achieve scale and market dominance
3. **Phase 3**: "Turn the screws" on monetization once position is secured

Carlson explicitly references this pattern:
> "That's exactly what Uber did. At first, Uber was focused on growth and they're highly unprofitable business. But Uber grew and grew and grew to scale. They seized the opportunity ahead of them and now they're highly profitable now that they've met scale."

#### Competitive Moat Analysis: The AI Threat Assessment

**The Bear Case (Addressed Head-On):**

Two primary concerns about AI competition:

**Concern 1: ChatGPT/AI Tutors Replace Duolingo**

Von Ahn's response:
> "The two things that people say about the competitive landscape with AI are number one, why would anybody want to learn a language with Duolingo when you can just learn it with Chacht? Um, okay. Uh, I, you know, we're not particularly worried about that. Um, you know, the we've said it before, the main thing that uh that we do really well, not only do we teach well, but the main thing that we do really well is keep people engaged. And in order to learn a language, you need to be engaged for years."

**The Moat Argument:**
- **Engagement, not content, is the competitive advantage**
- Language learning requires **years of daily practice**
- ChatGPT can provide lessons but cannot create **habit formation systems**
- Duolingo has curriculum designed for multi-year learning arcs

**Concern 2: Real-Time Translation Eliminates Learning Demand**

Von Ahn's response:
> "The other thing that people uh you know have said that they're worried about is oh well nobody's going to want to learn a language because um you know the we're going to have simultaneous language translation and okay. Um also not worried about that. Um, you know, I I believe in 100% of the Google IO conferences over the last 10 years, they have showcased simultaneous language translation. They do it every single year and it's good. It works. Uh, but this has been happening for the last 10 years and we have not seen the desire to learn a language go down at all. In fact, it has come up."

**The Refutation:**
- **Translation tech has existed for 10+ years** (Google Translate, etc.)
- **Demand for language learning has increased, not decreased**
- **Two primary user cohorts immune to translation:**
  1. **Hobby learners**: "It kind of doesn't matter whether a computer can do that. It's kind of the same with chess. By the way, computers are way better than humans at chess, but still, you know, we have millions of people wanting to learn chess"
  2. **English learners for job/immigration**: "People who are learning English and they actually want to learn English. Like that is, you know, for them, you know, being able to have like a phone that they have to hold out, it just kind of that's not what they what they want to do."

**Carlson's Assessment:**
> "I agree with this assessment. I think it's an honest assessment of the differences between Dualingo and just some AI learning app."

**Moat Summary:**
Duolingo's competitive advantage is **behavioral psychology and habit formation**, not content delivery. AI tools can match or exceed content quality, but cannot replicate:
- Multi-year curriculum design
- Gamification and reward systems refined over 10+ years
- Social features (streaks, leaderboards, friend competition)
- Brand trust and user base network effects

**Significance:** Duolingo is successfully expanding beyond language learning, proving the platform can extend to broader education. Chess having **better retention than language** validates the engagement moat applies to multiple categories.

If Carlson really believed in Duolingo, wouldn't it be a larger position? The 2% sizing suggests he doesn't actually have strong conviction.

**The Response:**

> "That shouldn't be interpreted as me being bearish on Duelingo, but I just believe that it's deserving of a smaller amount of capital. I think that's prudent investing."

> "I've taken in all of these risk factors and I assessed that this stock needed to be a smaller position and I sized it as a 2% position."

## $CMG (Chipotle) - "Fail of the Week" (Tertiary Focus)

### Main Thesis

**Operational excellence breakdown in fast-casual restaurant chain.** Chipotle is experiencing declining unit economics despite store expansion due to a fixable but unaddressed operational problem: wildly inconsistent portion sizes. Management is blaming macroeconomic factors rather than fixing controllable issues. No position; avoid until operational issues resolved.

**Strategy**: No position. Critical analysis of business problems.

---

### The Problem Analysis

#### Fundamental Evidence

**Stock Performance:**
> "Chipotle stock is down a lot... Chipotle is down around 50% on the year."

**Operating Metrics:**
> "The company's also struggling. For example, even though Chipotle is opening up more locations and they're seeing some growth that way, the average revenue per location is declining and it's been declining quarter after quarter."

**Key Metric:**
- **Unit count**: Growing (new store openings)
- **Revenue per unit**: **Declining** (quarter over quarter)

**Red Flag:** In a healthy restaurant chain, same-store sales should grow as brand matures and operations optimize. Declining revenue per location suggests:
- Customer traffic decreasing
- Average ticket size decreasing
- Customer satisfaction issues

#### The Root Cause: Portion Inconsistency

**The Social Proof:**

> "This is from Mike from Chipotle Tweets. He's tweeted that uh he he actually did kind of like a a little bit of a sting operation on Chipotle. He said that I ordered these burritos 15 minutes apart from the same store today. The one on the left I ordered in person and it's twice the size as the one on the right which was ordered online."

**The Viral Evidence:**
> "This has 10 million views, 144,000 hearts, 7,000 retweets. This is one of the most viral tweets across Twitter is people saying, 'I know this experience. I've also gone and ordered online and I get ripped off.'"

**The Professional Validation:**

> "Chipotle has even been sued for inconsistent portion sizes. The inconsistent portion sizes of Chipotle's food is such a big deal that one Wells Fargo analyst took it upon himself to do an in-depth study on the subject. He ordered a 100 burritos from all different locations at all different times. He had the exact same order every single time. The bowl weight varied widely. The lightest bowl was three standard deviations below the median."

**Statistical Significance:**
**3 standard deviations** = 99.7% confidence interval. This means the variation is **not random** - it's systematic operational failure.

#### Management's Response

**The Deflection:**

Management's earnings commentary:
> "Our third quarter performance fell short of our expectations due to persistent macroeconomic pressures."

**Carlson's Critique:**
> "And that certainly is a factor. I'll admit that it plays into any restaurant, but there's also a big factor that you do have control over. Even though the CEO does not have control over the economy, they do have control over how they run their restaurant."

**Previous CEO's "Solution":**

> "The previous CEO of Chipotle noticed that this is a problem. He even suggested going in person and then if you get a smaller portion size than what you believe you should have, if it's a kind of a smaller portion size than what you want, you give the employee the nod."

> "I don't believe that the customer should be the one responsible for making sure their burrito is made with the proportions that they're paying for. You shouldn't have to give them a nod to say, 'Hey, I really want the the full amount that I'm paying for.'"

### The Proposed Solution

**Carlson's Fix: Portion Scales**

**The Comparable Example:**

> "We have a guy here. He's doing a food review [of Mo'Bettahs Hawaiian restaurant]... There is one notable difference, one thing that is just different than what Chipotle does that makes it so at this restaurant, you get perfectly consistent portions of the protein every single time you go in."

**The Solution:**
> "That is a scale. That's a food scale. What Moveas does is they take your plate of food. Before they put any protein in it, they put it on the scale. They hit a button that zeros out the scale. Then they put food on to where you have the amount of food in weight that is the amount you're paying for."

**Implementation Analysis:**

**Pros:**
- **Low capex**: Food scales are inexpensive ($50-200 per station)
- **Easy to train**: Simple process (tare, add protein to target weight)
- **Solves consistency**: Eliminates primary customer complaint
- **Minimal throughput impact**: Adds ~3 seconds per order

**Cons:**
- **Cultural shift**: Employees must follow process (enforcement needed)
- **Speed perception**: Customers may perceive line moving slower
- **Portion creep**: If scales show employees are under-portioning, food costs could increase initially

**The Value Proposition:**

> "If you know what you're getting, you're going to go there more often."

**Economic Logic:**
- Inconsistency creates **negative surprise risk** (customer gambles on portion size)
- Negative experiences drive **permanent churn** (customers stop visiting)
- Consistency eliminates **viral complaints** (social media damage)
- Increased **frequency** drives same-store sales growth
