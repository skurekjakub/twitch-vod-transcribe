## 3.2. Phrases & Fixed Expressions

### Self-Introduction Phrases

**Phrase:** KawaJapa no Kyua Dorii desu  
**Romanization:** KawaJapa no Kyua Dorii desu  
**Literal:** "[I] am Cure Dolly of KawaJapa"  
**Meaning:** Self-introduction stating name and affiliation  
**Usage:** Used at the beginning of lessons as a natural self-introduction  
**Register:** Polite/Neutral  
**Note:** Demonstrates that "watashi wa" is unnecessary in context and would add unintended emphasis

---

**Phrase:** 私はさくらです  
**Romanization:** Watashi wa Sakura desu  
**Literal:** "As for me, [I] am Sakura"  
**Meaning:** I am Sakura  
**Usage:** Standard self-introduction with explicit topic marker  
**Register:** Polite/Neutral  
**Analysis:** Contains topic (watashi wa) + comment (ø ga Sakura desu)

---

**Phrase:** 私がさくらです  
**Romanization:** Watashi ga Sakura desu  
**Literal:** "I am Sakura" (with emphasis on "I")  
**Meaning:** *I* am Sakura (emphasizing identity)  
**Usage:** Used when identifying oneself, especially in response to a question like "Who is Sakura?"  
**Register:** Polite/Neutral  
**Analysis:** Uses logical particle ga, implying emphasis or contrast

---

### Travel/Movement Phrases

**Phrase:** 土曜日は東京に行きます  
**Romanization:** Doyoubi wa Toukyou ni ikimasu  
**Literal:** "As for Saturday, [I] go to Tokyo"  
**Meaning:** On Saturday, I'm going to Tokyo  
**Usage:** Stating plans with Saturday as the topic  
**Register:** Polite  
**Analysis:** Doyoubi wa (topic) + ø ga (implied subject) + Toukyou ni (destination) + ikimasu (verb)

---

**Phrase:** 東京は土曜日に行きます  
**Romanization:** Toukyou wa doyoubi ni ikimasu  
**Literal:** "As for Tokyo, [I] go [there] on Saturday"  
**Meaning:** As for Tokyo, I'm going there on Saturday  
**Usage:** Stating plans with Tokyo as the topic  
**Register:** Polite  
**Analysis:** Same logical action as above, but with different topicalization

---

### Lesson Closing Phrases

**Phrase:** これからよろしくお願いします  
**Romanization:** Kore kara yoroshiku onegai shimasu  
**Literal:** "From now on, please treat me favorably"  
**Meaning:** I look forward to our continued relationship / Thank you in advance  
**Usage:** Formal closing expression establishing ongoing relationship  
**Register:** Polite/Formal  
**Cultural note:** Commonly used when beginning a relationship or ongoing interaction

## 3.3. Collocations & Word Partnerships

### Particle + Noun Combinations

| Collocation | Romanization | Meaning | Notes |
|-------------|--------------|---------|-------|
| 東京に | Toukyou ni | To Tokyo | Direction/destination marker |
| 土曜日に | doyoubi ni | On Saturday | Time marker |
| 私が | watashi ga | I (as subject) | Logical subject marker |
| 私は | watashi wa | As for me | Topic marker |
| メアリーを | Mearii wo | Mary (as object) | Direct object marker |

### Verb + Particle Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| [place] に 行きます | Toukyou ni ikimasu | Go to [place] |
| [person] を [action] | Mearii wo kisu shimashita | Did [action] to [person] |
| [noun] です | Sakura desu | Is/am [noun] |

### Topic-Comment Structure Pattern

| Structure | Example | Analysis |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| [Topic] は [Comment] | Watashi wa Sakura desu | Topic (watashi wa) + Comment (ø ga Sakura desu) |
| [Topic] は [Destination] に [Verb] | Doyoubi wa Toukyou ni ikimasu | Topic (doyoubi wa) + Logical sentence (ø ga Toukyou ni ikimasu) |
