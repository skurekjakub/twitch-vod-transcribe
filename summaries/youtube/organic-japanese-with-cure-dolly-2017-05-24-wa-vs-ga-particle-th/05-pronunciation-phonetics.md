## 5.1. Sound Explanations

### Particle Pronunciation Notes

The video does not provide explicit pronunciation instruction, but the following can be inferred from the instructor's spoken examples:

#### The は (wa) Particle
- **Written:** は (ha in isolation)
- **Pronounced:** wa [わ]
- **Note:** The hiragana は is pronounced "ha" when used in words, but "wa" when used as a particle

#### The を (wo) Particle
- **Written:** を (wo)
- **Pronounced:** o [お] in modern standard Japanese
- **Note:** The instructor writes "wo" in romanization, reflecting the historical pronunciation, though modern pronunciation is simply "o"

#### The へ (he) Particle
- **Written:** へ (he in isolation)
- **Pronounced:** e [え]
- **Note:** Similar to は, this hiragana is pronounced differently when used as a particle versus in words

## 5.2. Intonation & Rhythm

### Observations from Instructor's Speech

While not explicitly taught, the instructor's delivery demonstrates:

1. **Sentence-final rising/falling patterns:** Standard Japanese declarative sentences end with falling intonation
2. **Particle attachment:** Particles are pronounced as part of the preceding word unit without pause
3. **です (desu) pronunciation:** The final "u" is often devoiced, sounding closer to "des"

### Topic-Comment Intonation

When demonstrating topic-comment structures, the instructor naturally shows:
- A slight pause or breath after the topic (は-marked element)
- The comment portion as a continuous thought unit

Example pattern:
```
土曜日は ↗ ... 東京に行きます ↘
(Topic)     (Comment)
```

## 5.3. Common Pronunciation Pitfalls

### Not Explicitly Covered

This lesson does not address specific pronunciation difficulties, as it focuses on grammatical structure. However, learners should be aware of:

1. **Particle sound changes:** は → wa, を → o, へ → e when functioning as particles
2. **Long vowels:** 東京 (Toukyou) contains a long "o" sound
3. **Devoiced vowels:** Final "u" in です and ます is typically devoiced
