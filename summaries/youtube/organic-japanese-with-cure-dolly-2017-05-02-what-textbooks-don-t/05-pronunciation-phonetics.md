## 5.2. Intonation & Rhythm

### General Observations

No explicit intonation instruction was provided in this lesson. However, the instructor's delivery demonstrates:

- **Even rhythm:** Japanese tends toward mora-timed rhythm, with each syllable (mora) given roughly equal time
- **Sentence-final falling intonation:** Declarative statements end with falling pitch
- **Particle pronunciation:** Particles are typically unstressed but clearly articulated

### Example Sentence Rhythm Patterns

The example sentences follow standard Japanese rhythmic patterns:

| Sentence | Mora Breakdown | Rhythm Pattern |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| 犬が走る | i-nu-ga-ha-shi-ru | 6 morae, even timing |
| 花が赤い | ha-na-ga-a-ka-i | 6 morae, even timing |
| うさぎがあります | u-sa-gi-ga-a-ri-ma-su | 8 morae, even timing |
| 鳥が飛んでいる | to-ri-ga-to-n-de-i-ru | 8 morae (ん counts as one mora) |

## 5.3. Common Pronunciation Pitfalls

While not explicitly discussed in this lesson, the vocabulary presented includes sounds that commonly challenge English-speaking learners:

### Potentially Challenging Sounds

| Sound | Example | Challenge for English Speakers |
|-------|---------|--------------------------------|
| つ (tsu) | 疲れた (tsukareta) | The /ts/ combination at the start of a syllable is uncommon in English |
| ん (n) | 飛んでいる (tonde iru) | The moraic nasal ん is pronounced differently depending on the following sound |
| り (ri) | あります (arimasu) | Japanese 'r' is a flap, between English 'r', 'l', and 'd' |
| は as particle | 私は (watashi wa) | Written は but pronounced わ (wa) when used as the topic particle |

### は vs わ Distinction

An important pronunciation note implicit in the lesson:
- **Character:** は
- **As a character in words:** pronounced "ha"
- **As the topic particle:** pronounced "wa"

This is demonstrated in the example 「私は日本人です」 where は is the topic particle and pronounced "wa," not "ha."
