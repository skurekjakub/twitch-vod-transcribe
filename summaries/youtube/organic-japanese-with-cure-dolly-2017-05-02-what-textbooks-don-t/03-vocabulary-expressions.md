## 3.2. Phrases & Fixed Expressions

### Opening Expression

**Phrase:** KawaJapa no Kyua Dorii (KawaJapaのキュアドリー)
**Literal:** "Cure Dolly of KawaJapa"
**Meaning:** Self-introduction identifying the speaker as Cure Dolly from the KawaJapa channel/organization
**Usage:** Used as an opening greeting/identification at the start of lessons
**Register:** Neutral/informal
**Notes:** This uses the の (no) possessive particle to indicate affiliation

---

### Closing Expression

**Phrase:** よろしくお願いします (Yoroshiku onegai shimasu)
**Literal:** "Please treat [me/this] favorably"
**Meaning:** A versatile polite expression used to request continued goodwill, support, or favorable treatment; in this context, asking viewers to continue following the lesson series
**Usage:** Used when:
- Meeting someone new
- Beginning a working relationship
- Asking for someone's help or cooperation
- Closing a presentation or lesson (requesting continued engagement)
**Register:** Polite/formal
**Variations:** 
- よろしく (yoroshiku) - casual, among friends
- よろしくお願いいたします (yoroshiku onegai itashimasu) - more formal/humble

---

### Class Dismissal

**Phrase:** Class dismissed
**Meaning:** Signal that the lesson has ended
**Usage:** Spoken in English at the end of the video to mark the conclusion of the lesson
**Register:** Neutral (English phrase used in educational context)
**Notes:** Used playfully to maintain a classroom atmosphere in the video format

## 3.3. Collocations & Word Partnerships

### Particle + Noun Combinations

The lesson emphasizes the fundamental collocation pattern in Japanese:

| Pattern | Japanese | Romanization | Meaning |
|---------|----------|--------------|---------|
| Noun + が | 犬が | inu ga | the dog (as subject) |
| Noun + が | 花が | hana ga | the flower (as subject) |
| Noun + が | うさぎが | usagi ga | the rabbit (as subject) |
| Noun + が | 鳥が | tori ga | the bird (as subject) |
| Noun + が | 私が | watashi ga | I (as subject) |
| Noun + は | 私は | watashi wa | I (as topic) |

### Verb Conjugation Patterns Demonstrated

| Base Form | Conjugated Form | Pattern | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|---------|
| 飛ぶ (tobu) | 飛んでいる (tonde iru) | te-form + いる | ongoing action "is flying" |
| 疲れる (tsukareru) | 疲れた (tsukareta) | past tense | completed action "became tired" |
| ある (aru) | あります (arimasu) | masu-form | polite "exists/there is" |
