## Essential Concepts to Internalize

### The One Rule That Governs All Japanese Sentences

**Core Concept:**
Every Japanese sentence consists of exactly two essential elements connected by が:

```
[Subject/主語] + が + [Predicate/述語]
```

This is the **single most important takeaway** from this lesson.

## Essential Vocabulary to Memorize

### Grammatical Terms

| Japanese | Romanization | Meaning | Priority |
|----------|--------------|---------|----------|
| 主語 | shugo | subject | Essential |
| 述語 | jutsugo | predicate | Essential |
| が | ga | subject-marking particle | Essential |
| は | wa | topic-marking particle | Essential (next lesson) |

## Grammar Structures to Practice

### Structure 1: Basic Subject-Predicate with が

**Pattern:** [Noun] が [Verb/Adjective]

**Practice creating sentences like:**
- [Animal] が [action verb]
- [Thing] が [descriptive adjective]
- [Thing] が あります/います

### Structure 2: Recognizing the "Logical が"

**Skill to develop:** When you see a sentence without visible が, mentally identify:
1. Who/what is the implied subject?
2. Where would the が logically go?

## Suggested Next Steps for Learners

### Immediate Actions

1. **Review the example sentences**
   - 犬が走る (Inu ga hashiru) - "The dog runs"
   - 花が赤い (Hana ga akai) - "The flower is red"
   - うさぎがあります (Usagi ga arimasu) - "There is a rabbit"
   - 鳥が飛んでいる (Tori ga tonde iru) - "The bird is flying"

2. **Practice sentence analysis**
   - Take any simple Japanese sentence
   - Identify the subject (主語)
   - Find the が particle (visible or logical)
   - Identify the predicate (述語)

3. **Create your own sentences**
   - Write 5-10 simple sentences following the [Subject] が [Predicate] pattern
   - Use vocabulary you know

### Short-Term Actions

4. **Watch the next lesson**
   - The は (wa) particle lesson is essential for understanding how は and が work together
   - This will resolve the question raised about 「私は日本人です」

5. **Consider reading "Unlocking Japanese"**
   - The instructor's book provides deeper explanation of these concepts
   - Described as readable in "one or two evenings"

### Long-Term Practice

6. **Apply this analysis to all Japanese you encounter**
   - When reading or listening to Japanese, actively identify the Subject-が-Predicate structure
   - Build the habit of seeing Japanese sentences through this structural lens

7. **Build particle awareness**
   - Pay special attention to particles in everything you read
   - Particles are "the glue that holds Japanese together"
