## 3.2. Grammatical/Linguistic Terms

| Target Language | Romanization | Translation | Explanation |
|-----------------|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| ゼロ代名詞 | zero daimeishi | zero pronoun | The invisible pronoun that is understood from context |
| が | ga | subject marker particle | Marks the grammatical subject; called the "center/fulcrum" of every sentence |
| を | wo (o) | object marker particle | Marks the direct object of a verb |
| に | ni | location/indirect object particle | One of the basic one-character particles |
| は | wa | topic marker particle | Mentioned as upcoming topic; distinct from subject marker |
| 助詞 | joshi | particle | Postpositional markers attached to nouns |
| 名詞 | meishi | noun | Words representing things, people, concepts |
| 代名詞 | daimeishi | pronoun | Words substituting for nouns |

## 3.3. Phrases & Fixed Expressions

---

**Phrase:** KawaJapa no Kyua Dorii desu (かわジャパのキュアドリーです)

**Literal:** "KawaJapa's Cure Dolly am"

**Meaning:** "I am Cure Dolly from KawaJapa"

**Usage:** Self-introduction pattern using the possessive の (no) particle and です (desu) copula

**Register:** Polite/formal

**Structure Breakdown:**
- KawaJapa = Organization/channel name
- の (no) = Possessive particle ("of/from")
- Kyua Dorii = Name (Cure Dolly)
- です (desu) = Polite copula ("am/is")

---

**Phrase:** ミルクを持っていますか (miruku wo motte imasu ka)

**Literal:** "Milk [object] holding are [question]?"

**Meaning:** "Do you have milk?" / "Got milk?"

**Usage:** Polite question asking about possession; demonstrates zero pronoun (あなたが is implied)

**Register:** Polite (います/imasu form)

**Full Explicit Form:** (あなたが) ミルクを持っていますか – (anata ga) miruku wo motte imasu ka

**Structure Breakdown:**
- ∅が (zero pronoun + ga) = invisible "you" as subject
- ミルク (miruku) = milk
- を (wo) = object marker
- 持って (motte) = te-form of 持つ (to have/hold)
- います (imasu) = polite progressive/stative auxiliary
- か (ka) = question particle

---

**Phrase:** 誰がケーキを食べた？ (dare ga keeki wo tabeta?)

**Literal:** "Who [subject] cake [object] ate?"

**Meaning:** "Who ate the cake?"

**Usage:** Question using interrogative pronoun 誰 (dare) in subject position

**Register:** Casual (た-form without です)

**Structure Breakdown:**
- 誰 (dare) = who
- が (ga) = subject marker
- ケーキ (keeki) = cake
- を (wo) = object marker
- 食べた (tabeta) = past tense of 食べる (to eat)

---

**Phrase:** 私が食べた (watashi ga tabeta)

**Literal:** "I [subject] ate"

**Meaning:** "I ate (it)" – where "it" (the cake) is represented by the zero pronoun with を

**Usage:** Response to "Who ate the cake?" demonstrating zero pronoun for the object

**Register:** Casual

**Full Explicit Form:** 私がケーキを食べた (watashi ga keeki wo tabeta)

**Structure Breakdown:**
- 私 (watashi) = I
- が (ga) = subject marker
- ∅を (zero pronoun + wo) = invisible "it/the cake" as object
- 食べた (tabeta) = ate

---

**Phrase:** これからよろしくお願いします (kore kara yoroshiku onegai shimasu)

**Literal:** "From now on, please treat me well / please be kind to me"

**Meaning:** Standard closing phrase expressing hope for a good ongoing relationship; here used as "I look forward to continuing with you"

**Usage:** Common phrase used when beginning a relationship, starting a new endeavor, or at the end of introductory content

**Register:** Polite/formal

**Structure Breakdown:**
- これから (kore kara) = from now on, from here on
- よろしく (yoroshiku) = please (treat me) well/favorably
- お願いします (onegai shimasu) = I request/please

## 3.4. Collocations & Word Partnerships

### Verb + Particle Combinations

| Verb | Particle Pattern | Example | Meaning |
|------|-----------------|---------|---------|
| 食べる (taberu) | [noun]を食べる | ケーキを食べる | to eat [something] |
| 持つ (motsu) | [noun]を持つ | ミルクを持つ | to have/hold [something] |

### Particle + Noun Units

The lesson emphasizes that particles and nouns form **inseparable units**. Key combinations from the video:

| Noun + Particle | Romanization | Function |
|-----------------|--------------|----------|
| 私が | watashi ga | "I" as subject |
| 誰が | dare ga | "who" as subject |
| ミルクを | miruku wo | "milk" as object |
| ケーキを | keeki wo | "cake" as object |

### Question Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Function |
|---------|---------|----------|
| [sentence] + か | 持っていますか | Marks sentence as a question |
| 誰が + [verb]? | 誰が食べた？ | "Who did [verb]?" |

## 3.5. Loan Words (外来語 / Gairaigo)

Both content nouns used as examples in this lesson are loan words from English:

| Japanese | Origin | Original Word | Notes |
|----------|--------|---------------|-------|
| ミルク (miruku) | English | milk | Written in katakana; alternative: 牛乳 (gyuunyuu) for "cow's milk" |
| ケーキ (keeki) | English | cake | Written in katakana; commonly used for Western-style cakes |
