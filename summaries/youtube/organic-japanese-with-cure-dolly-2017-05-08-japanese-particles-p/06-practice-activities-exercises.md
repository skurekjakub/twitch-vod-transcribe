# 6. Practice Activities & Exercises

## Overview

This lesson is primarily conceptual/theoretical and does not include formal practice activities or drills. However, the examples provided by the instructor can be converted into useful practice exercises for learners to reinforce the zero pronoun concept.

---

## 6.1. Zero Pronoun Identification Exercise

**Activity Type:** Analysis / Identification

**Instructions:** For each Japanese sentence below, identify:
1. Whether there is a zero pronoun
2. What the zero pronoun represents
3. What particle the zero pronoun carries

**Practice Sentences (from the lesson):**

### Sentence 1
```
ミルクを持っていますか
miruku wo motte imasu ka
```

**Questions:**
- Is there a zero pronoun? 
- What does it represent?
- What particle does it carry?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

- **Yes**, there is a zero pronoun
- It represents **あなた (anata)** – "you"
- It carries **が (ga)** – subject marker
- Full form: **∅が** ミルクを持っていますか (You have milk?)

</details>

---

### Sentence 2
```
私が食べた
watashi ga tabeta
```
(In response to "誰がケーキを食べた？" – "Who ate the cake?")

**Questions:**
- Is there a zero pronoun?
- What does it represent?
- What particle does it carry?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

- **Yes**, there is a zero pronoun
- It represents **ケーキ (keeki)** – "cake"
- It carries **を (wo)** – object marker
- Full form: 私が **∅を** 食べた (I ate [it])

</details>

## 6.2. English-to-Japanese Comparison Exercise

**Activity Type:** Contrastive Analysis

**Instructions:** Compare how English and Japanese handle the same information. Fill in what's explicit vs. implicit in each language.

### Exercise Set

| English | What's explicit | Japanese | What's implicit |
|---------|-----------------|----------|-----------------|
| "Do **you** have milk?" | "you" | ミルクを持っていますか | ? |
| "**I** ate **it**" | "I", "it" | 私が食べた | ? |
| "**She** opened **her** door" | "she", "her" | ドアを開けた | ? |

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

| English | Explicit | Japanese | Implicit |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|
| "Do you have milk?" | "you" | ミルクを持っていますか | あなたが (you-SUBJ) |
| "I ate it" | "I", "it" | 私が食べた | ケーキを (cake-OBJ) |
| "She opened her door" | "she", "her" | ドアを開けた | 彼女が (she-SUBJ), 彼女の (her) |

</details>

## 6.3. Fill-in-the-Particle Exercise

**Activity Type:** Fill-in-the-blank

**Instructions:** Fill in the appropriate particle for the zero pronoun. Context is provided.

### Exercise 1
**Context:** Someone asks you what you're drinking. You answer:

```
コーヒーを飲んでいます
(koohii wo nonde imasu)
"(I) am drinking coffee"
```

**Question:** The zero pronoun represents "I" (私). What invisible particle does it carry?

**Answer:** ∅**が** – because "I" is the subject

---

### Exercise 2
**Context:** You ask "Did you see the movie?" and your friend answers:

```
見た
(mita)
"(I) saw (it)"
```

**Question:** There are TWO zero pronouns here. Identify both and their particles.

**Answer:** 
- ∅**が** = 私 (I) – subject
- ∅**を** = 映画 (the movie) – object

## 6.4. Sentence Expansion Exercise

**Activity Type:** Expansion / Reconstruction

**Instructions:** Expand these minimal Japanese sentences into their full explicit forms.

### Exercise Set

| Minimal | Full Form (Fill in) |
|---------|---------------------|
| 食べた (tabeta) | ____が ____を 食べた |
| 持っていますか (motte imasu ka) | ____が ____を 持っていますか |
| 飲んだ (nonda) | ____が ____を 飲んだ |

**Context for Answers:**
- 食べた: Response to "Who ate the sushi?" – "I ate (it)"
- 持っていますか: "Do you have a pen?"
- 飲んだ: Response to "Did she drink the tea?" – "(She) drank (it)"

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

| Minimal | Full Form |
|---------|-----------|
| 食べた | **私が 寿司を** 食べた |
| 持っていますか | **あなたが ペンを** 持っていますか |
| 飲んだ | **彼女が お茶を** 飲んだ |

</details>

## 6.5. Comprehension Check Questions

**Activity Type:** Self-assessment / Review

**Instructions:** Answer these questions based on the lesson content.

1. **Why is the ga-particle (が) said to be in "every Japanese sentence"?**

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
Because even when が is not visible, it exists invisibly attached to the zero pronoun. Every sentence has a subject, and every subject has が – even if that subject is the invisible zero pronoun.
</details>

2. **How is the Japanese zero pronoun different from English pronoun omission (e.g., "Got milk?")?**

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
In English, "Got milk?" is considered colloquial/informal/ungrammatical – proper English requires "Have you got milk?" or "Do you have milk?" In Japanese, using the zero pronoun is standard, correct grammar – not informal or sloppy.
</details>

3. **Can the zero pronoun carry particles other than が?**

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
Yes. The zero pronoun carries whatever particle its referent would carry. If it represents an object, it carries を. If it represents an indirect object, it carries に. The particle depends on the grammatical role.
</details>

4. **Is the zero pronoun more ambiguous than English "it"?**

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>
No. As the instructor explains, "it" in English can refer to almost anything (the Andromeda galaxy, a grain of sand, a flower, etc.). Both "it" and the zero pronoun derive their meaning entirely from context. They are equally ambiguous/unambiguous.
</details>

## 6.6. Suggested Self-Study Activities

Based on the lesson, learners could practice by:

1. **Reading Practice:** Take a Japanese text and highlight every sentence that appears to lack a subject. Identify the zero pronoun and determine what it represents.

2. **Writing Practice:** Write simple Japanese sentences, then strip them down to their minimal form using zero pronouns. Then reconstruct them.

3. **Listening Practice:** Listen to Japanese conversations and try to identify when speakers use zero pronouns. What would the explicit form be?

4. **Research Activity:** Google "Japanese zero pronoun" as the instructor suggests to find academic papers discussing this phenomenon in more depth.
