# Module 2: Episode Index & Glossary

## 2.1. Primary Subjects & Themes

- **The Waterloo co-op program model** and its impact on career development through iterative learning
- **Non-linear career paths**: oscillating between IC (Individual Contributor) and management roles
- **"Be the only, not the best" philosophy**: building a talent stack vs. hyper-specialization
- **The nature of principal and distinguished engineer roles**: requirements, expectations, and characteristics
- **Dynamic range as a success factor**: technical versatility and execution flexibility
- **Tour of duty thinking**: commitment to problems rather than permanent role definitions
- **PostRank founding story**: from academic pivot to startup to Google acquisition
- **Career transitions at Google**: from manager to IC and back to management
- **Google's "Make the Web Fast" initiative** and web performance optimization
- **Web 2.0 revolution and social engagement metrics** as alternatives to traditional PageRank
- **The acquisition process**: how tech companies evaluate and integrate startup teams
- **Measuring web performance** and web standards work (W3C and IETF)
- **Developer relations as a go-to-market strategy** for platform adoption
- **Overcoming career-stage filtering and fear of public output**
- **Educational philosophy inspired by Sir Ken Robinson**: moving beyond factory-model education

## 2.2. Glossary of People, Places & Passing Mentions

**Ilia Gregoric**: The guest being interviewed; distinguished engineer at Shopify (VP-level role), former Google engineer, founder of PostRank.

**Ryan Peterman**: The podcast host conducting the interview.

**University of Waterloo**: Canadian university known for its co-op program, where students alternate between academic semesters and work terms, allowing for six internship experiences during their degree.

**MIT, Caltech, and Tsinghua University (China)**: Universities mentioned by Ryan as sources of the "smartest" interns he worked with, contrasted with Waterloo's "best" interns.

**PostRank**: Ilia's startup company that built a social engagement ranking algorithm (an evolution of Google's PageRank concept for the Web 2.0 era). Acquired by Google in 2011.

**University of Toronto**: Where Ilia was accepted for graduate school but ultimately deferred and never completed due to starting PostRank.

**Larry and Sergey (Larry Page and Sergey Brin)**: Co-founders of Google, mentioned in context of the original PageRank algorithm using links as social currency.

**Phil Moody**: Lead Product Manager on Google Analytics who initiated acquisition conversations with PostRank at a Bay Area conference.

**Google Analytics**: The Google product division where Ilia and the PostRank team were integrated after acquisition.

**Google+**: Google's social network initiative (now sunset), launched as a competitive response to Facebook. Context: PostRank's acquisition was partly driven by Google's need to accelerate social features.

**Facebook**: Mentioned as Google's competitor during the "battle lines for social" period when Google was acquiring social-related technology and teams.

**"Make the Web Fast"**: A skunk works project at Google started by Sergey (Brin), focused on various initiatives to improve internet performance—from radio towers to TCP/IP optimization to proxy optimization.

**W3C (World Wide Web Consortium) and IETF (Internet Engineering Task Force)**: Standards bodies where Ilia contributed to defining web performance metrics for browsers.

**Shopify**: Canadian e-commerce company where Ilia currently serves as distinguished engineer.

**Sir Ken Robinson**: Author and education philosopher whose work on rethinking education systems and creativity deeply influenced Ilia's career philosophy. Notable works include "Finding Your Element" and "Creative Schools." He is known for highly-rated TED talks on education reform.

**Bigtable and Hadoop**: Distributed data storage technologies mentioned in context of Google's internal infrastructure being generations ahead of published academic papers.

**PageRank**: Google's original algorithm for ranking web pages based on link structure, treated as social currency between pages.

**Web 2.0**: The evolution of the internet from static web pages to dynamic, user-generated content including blogs, comments, and social interactions.

**Reddit**: Social platform mentioned as an example of where conversations happen about content, generating social engagement signals.

**Bay Area / San Francisco / Mountain View**: Geographic locations mentioned—San Francisco where PostRank presented at a conference, Mountain View where Google's headquarters is located.

**Homer Simpson meme (disappearing into bushes)**: Pop culture reference Ilia uses to describe successfully making himself unnecessary to a project team.

**Wall Street**: Mentioned as one career path that Waterloo co-op students tried and sometimes rejected during their exploratory phases.
