## 3.2. Phrases & Fixed Expressions

### Opening Expression

**Phrase:** KawaJapa no Kyua Dorii desu (KawaJapa の キュア・ドリー です)
**Literal:** "KawaJapa's Cure Dolly am"
**Meaning:** "I am Cure Dolly from KawaJapa"
**Usage:** Self-introduction at the beginning of the lesson
**Register:** Polite
**Variations:** Standard self-introduction pattern: [Organization] の [Name] です

---

### Closing Expression

**Phrase:** これから よろしく お願いします (Kore kara yoroshiku onegai shimasu)
**Literal:** "From now on, please treat me well"
**Meaning:** A polite expression used to establish goodwill, roughly meaning "Please be good to me from now on" or "I look forward to our continued relationship"
**Usage:** Used as a closing remark to establish ongoing rapport with the audience
**Register:** Polite/Formal
**Variations:** 
- よろしく (yoroshiku) - casual version
- よろしくお願いいたします (yoroshiku onegai itashimasu) - more formal/humble

---

### Example Sentences from the Lesson

**Phrase:** 私は日本人です (Watashi wa Nihonjin desu)
**Literal:** "As for me, (I) am a Japanese person"
**Meaning:** "I am Japanese"
**Usage:** Self-identification, stating nationality
**Register:** Polite
**Structure:** [Topic]は [zero pronoun が] [noun] です
**Note:** The ga-marked subject is the invisible "I" that is defined by the wa-marked topic

---

**Phrase:** 私はうなぎです (Watashi wa unagi desu)
**Literal:** "As for me, (it) is an eel"
**Meaning:** "I will have eel" / "As for me, it's eel" (in restaurant context)
**Usage:** Ordering food at a restaurant
**Register:** Polite
**Structure:** [Topic]は [zero pronoun が] [noun] です
**Note:** The ga-marked subject is NOT "watashi" but rather the implicit "it" (what I'm ordering)

---

**Phrase:** これはペンです (Kore wa pen desu)
**Literal:** "As for this, (it) is a pen"
**Meaning:** "This is a pen"
**Usage:** Identifying objects
**Register:** Polite
**Structure:** [Topic]は [zero pronoun が] [noun] です

---

**Phrase:** 土曜日は東京に行きます (Doyoubi wa Toukyou ni ikimasu)
**Literal:** "As for Saturday, (I) go to Tokyo"
**Meaning:** "I'm going to Tokyo on Saturday" (with Saturday as the topic)
**Usage:** Stating plans with a time topic
**Register:** Polite
**Note:** The topic (Saturday) is not the subject; the invisible "I" is the subject

---

**Phrase:** 東京は土曜日に行きます (Toukyou wa doyoubi ni ikimasu)
**Literal:** "As for Tokyo, (I) go on Saturday"
**Meaning:** "I'm going to Tokyo on Saturday" (with Tokyo as the topic)
**Usage:** Stating plans with a destination topic
**Register:** Polite
**Note:** Same logical meaning as the previous sentence, but different emphasis/topic

---

## 3.3. Collocations & Word Partnerships

### Particle Combinations

| Pattern | Romanization | Usage | Example |
|---------|--------------|-------|---------|
| [noun]は...です | [noun] wa ... desu | Topic + predicate pattern | 私は日本人です |
| [noun]が...です | [noun] ga ... desu | Subject + predicate pattern | (zero) が ペンです |
| [place]に行きます | [place] ni ikimasu | Going to a location | 東京に行きます |
| [time]に | [time] ni | At a specific time | 土曜日に |

### Common Grammatical Patterns

- **[Topic] は [Zero Pronoun が] [Noun] です:** The fundamental sentence structure demonstrated throughout the lesson
  - Example: 私は (Ø が) うなぎです - "As for me, (it) is eel"

- **[Topic] は [Zero Pronoun が] [Place] に 行きます:** Topic-marked sentence with movement verb
  - Example: 土曜日は (Ø が) 東京に行きます - "As for Saturday, (I) go to Tokyo"

### Technical Grammatical Terms Used

| English Term | Japanese Equivalent | Explanation |
|--------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Topic (marker) | (wa particle function) | What the sentence is "about" |
| Subject (marker) | (ga particle function) | The doer or be-er of the action |
| Zero pronoun | ゼロ代名詞 (zero daimeishi) | Invisible pronoun carrying ga |
| Grammatical subject | 文法的主語 (bunpouteki shugo) | The actual doer of the action |
