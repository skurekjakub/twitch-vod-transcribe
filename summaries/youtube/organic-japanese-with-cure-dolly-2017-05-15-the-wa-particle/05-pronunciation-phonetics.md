## 5.1. Sound Explanations

### The は Particle Pronunciation

**Key Point:** The hiragana は is normally pronounced "ha," but when used as the topic-marking particle, it is pronounced **"wa"**.

| Context | Hiragana | Pronunciation |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| As a syllable | は | ha |
| As the topic particle | は | wa |

This is one of the few cases in Japanese where a hiragana character has an irregular pronunciation based on grammatical function.

## 5.3. Common Pronunciation Pitfalls

### Implied from the Lesson Content

While not explicitly discussed as pronunciation issues, the lesson implies awareness of these points:

1. **Distinguishing は as "wa" (particle) vs. "ha" (syllable):**
   - Learners must recognize when は functions as the topic particle and pronounce it as "wa"
   - Example: 私**は** (watashi **wa**) - the は is pronounced "wa"

2. **Long vowels in place names:**
   - 東京 (Toukyou) contains a long "o" sound (ou → ō)
   - Proper pronunciation maintains this vowel length
