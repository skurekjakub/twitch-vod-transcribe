## 6. Investment Approach Discussions

### Value Investing

- **Core Concept**: Buying shares when the underlying asset (particularly drugs in biotech context) is undervalued relative to purchase price. Quote: "When you buy a company stock, you're buying... a portion of the company. And the asset of the company in the case of a drug company is a drug. And sometimes, at least my experience, I buy shares of the drug companies when I look at the drug and I think it's undervalued relative to what I'm paying for."

- **Key Insights**: 
  - Asset-based valuation particularly applicable to biotech/pharma
  - Requires deep understanding of underlying assets (drugs, technology, etc.)
  - Not applicable to all situations - must understand what creates value

- **Application**: Applied to Spruce Biosciences position - explicitly states investment is NOT based on FDA approval catalyst, but on underlying drug asset valuation

### Momentum Trading

- **Core Concept**: Trading based on price trends and technical patterns

- **Key Insights**: "Trading momentum works pretty well as long as you have an itchy trigger finger to get out when it goes against you"

- **Application**: 
  - Acknowledged as valid strategy but requires strict discipline
  - Quick exit discipline is critical to success
  - Speaker admits personal weakness: "Usually what happens is when I cover the short that I was shorting, I shouldn't have covered it because it's actually going down fast"

### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Analysis

- **Core Concept**: Valuing companies based on present value of future cash flows. Most important question: "What is the steady state earnings?"

- **Key Insights**:
  - **Steady State Definition**: "Many companies are not in a steady state and some companies by definition will never be in a steady state" - also called "maturity"
  - **Challenge for NVIDIA**: Planned obsolescence product cycle (V100→A100→H100→B200→Ruben) makes steady state analysis extremely difficult
  - **Coca-Cola Contrast**: Mature companies with predictable growth easier to model using "area under the curve" calculus approach
  - **Semiconductor Complexity**: Must account for:
    - Product cycles
    - Business cycles  
    - Technology cycles
  - **Critical Mistake Warning**: "You mistake cyclicality for permanent growth... one of the biggest mistakes you can make"

- **Application**: 
  - Active DCF modeling shown for NVIDIA (building spreadsheet with EV/Earnings, P/E, Revenue Growth, EV/Sales)
  - Historical Intel analysis to understand semiconductor cyclical patterns
  - Acknowledgment that NVIDIA DCF requires "creative thinking" due to complexity
