## 6. Investment Approach Discussions

### Fundamental Analysis Methodology

- **Core Concept**: "To do fundamental analysis is to look at the details of the company and to try to value the company the best you can. I mean, that's really what fundamental analysis is."

- **Key Insights**: 
  - Uses detailed balance sheet analysis (demonstrated with Berkshire deep-dive)
  - Focuses on debt classification nuances (insurance float as debt)
  - Revenue/burn rate analysis (Biohaven burning $600M annually)
  - Management track record heavily weighted (Vlad's $14B exit)
  - Portfolio value in multi-asset companies (downside protection)
  - Uses EV/EBITDA multiples for valuation frameworks

- **Application**: 
  - Live balance sheet build for Berkshire showing methodology
  - Biohaven analysis incorporated 70-page JP Morgan deck
  - Cross-referencing sell-side research (UBS, JP Morgan, Bernstein)
  - FOIA request monitoring for information edge
  - Domain registration checks for signal detection

---

### FDA Approval Event Trading / Biotech Catalysts

- **Core Concept**: Binary FDA decisions create asymmetric risk/reward opportunities, especially when market expectations are low

- **Key Insights**: 
  - "The chances are probably higher than perceived" for Biohaven despite weak Phase 3 data
  - Real-world evidence can overcome controlled trial failures in rare diseases
  - Management credibility and lobbying can influence regulatory outcomes
  - Options provide leverage for binary events (5x-6x payoffs possible)
  - Even on failure, strong pipeline companies can recover
  - Medical community support ("doctors want it") matters for orphan drugs
  - PDUFA date monitoring and document scraping can provide edge

- **Application**: 
  - Biohaven positioned as "buy a little" with potential to add on weakness
  - Options strategy: $20 calls at $5, $35 calls at $2 for asymmetric payoff
  - Building FDA document monitoring system (web scraping, FOIA tracking)
  - Checking brand name domain registrations for approval signals
  - Understanding AdCom dynamics (either very good or very bad signal)

### Hedging / Pair Trading

- **Core Concept**: Expressing directional views through long/short pairs rather than naked positions

- **Key Insights**: 
  - "If I buy Meta, I'd have to short something in its place, like Apple"
  - Relative value more important than absolute in toppy markets
  - Apple "really expensive" vs. Meta on "value basis" creates pair opportunity
  - Reduces market beta exposure while maintaining sector/factor exposure

- **Application**: 
  - Meta long / Apple short pair discussed as current opportunity
  - Would implement if entering Meta position
  - Acknowledges market timing risk: "I also wonder if the market's going to [crash]"
