# 2. Episode Index & Glossary

## 2.1. Primary Subjects & Themes

* **Velodata platform navigation and interface overview** - Core focus on explaining the five main sections and how to access different features.

* **TradFi section functionality** - CME (Chicago Mercantile Exchange) data visualization for Bitcoin and Ethereum futures and options.

* **Market section features** - Real-time tracking of cryptocurrency price changes, open interest, funding rates, and volume across multiple timeframes.

* **Options data analysis** - Interface for viewing options contracts, implied volatility, Greeks, and most traded options.

* **Futures market analysis** - Detailed analytics for individual cryptocurrency futures contracts including liquidations, funding rates, and volume distribution.

* **Chart customization and technical analysis** - Primary focus on orderflow indicators, cumulative delta (CVD), spot CVD, and multi-chart layouts.

* **Watchlist creation and management** - Creating custom cryptocurrency lists with configurable columns for monitoring specific metrics.

* **Platform customization options** - Visual settings (light/dark mode, color schemes) and data display preferences (dollars vs. coins).

* **Mobile compatibility and accessibility** - The platform's functionality on mobile devices and bookmark-to-home-screen capability.

* **Orderflow indicators for day trading** - Specific focus on volume analysis, liquidations, open interest, and funding rate indicators.

## 2.2. Glossary of People, Places & Passing Mentions

**Runner XBT:** The video presenter and tutorial creator. Identifies themselves as a day trader who primarily utilizes orderflow indicators and the Chart section of Velodata. Explicitly states they are not affiliated with or paid by Velodata.

**Velodata:** The cryptocurrency trading analytics platform being demonstrated. The presenter mentions it has official email addresses, verified Twitter accounts, and comprehensive documentation available for users.

**CME (Chicago Mercantile Exchange):** Traditional financial exchange mentioned in the TradFi section. Velodata displays CME data for Bitcoin and Ethereum futures and options, including open interest, volume, and annualized basis.

**Binance:** Cryptocurrency exchange referenced multiple times. Velodata shows "binance usdt futures" and the presenter notes that in examples (such as Solana), "most volume is done on binance."

**Bitcoin:** Primary cryptocurrency used for most demonstration examples throughout the tutorial, particularly in the TradFi and Chart sections.

**Ethereum (ETH):** Secondary cryptocurrency mentioned as an option in the TradFi section alongside Bitcoin for CME data viewing.

**Solana (SOL):** Used as a specific example when demonstrating the Futures section. The presenter describes it as "a currently popular coin" and shows it categorized as a "layer one" blockchain. Also used to demonstrate session-based return analysis.

**GBTC (Grayscale Bitcoin Trust):** Mentioned in the TradFi section as one of the six windows showing "gbtc premium" data alongside other CME metrics.

**TradingView:** Referenced when the presenter describes the Chart section's layout, stating "this is the classic trading view layout," indicating the platform uses familiar charting conventions.

**BRC20:** A token category/standard mentioned briefly when demonstrating how Velodata shows coin categories. Used as an example contrast to Solana's "layer one" categorization.

**API Access:** Referenced multiple times as available through Velodata. The presenter mentions an API button on the main page and shows API access buttons above charts, though states they are "not the developer" when declining to explain API details.

**Greeks:** Options trading metrics mentioned in the Options section. The presenter notes "you can add Greeks over here" but doesn't elaborate since they identify as not being an options trader.

**Implied Volatility (IV):** Options metric referenced in the Options section as available data within Velodata, shown in the context of options analysis windows.

**US Session:** Trading session mentioned when analyzing Solana's returns by session, where the presenter notes "Solana went up mostly during us session" in a 7-day analysis.

**Layer One:** Blockchain categorization term used to describe Solana's category within Velodata's classification system, contrasted with other categories like BRC20.

**USDT Futures:** Specific futures contract type mentioned, referring to Tether (stablecoin) margined futures contracts on exchanges like Binance.

**FDV (Fully Diluted Valuation):** Cryptocurrency valuation metric shown alongside market cap in the Futures section for individual coins.
