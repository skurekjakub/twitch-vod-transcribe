## 3. Strategies & Theses

### Buyback Announcement Trading
**Logic:** Companies announcing significant share repurchase programs create immediate buying pressure and signal management confidence in valuation  
**Execution:** Quick entries pre-market or at open with tight trailing stop losses (3% for CXW)  
**Examples:** CXW ($200M buyback), Celsius ($300M buyback on spot market, not pre-arranged)  
**Risk Management:** Tight stops due to potential "exit liquidity" concerns; ready to take quick profits

### Healthcare Dip-Buying on Political Uncertainty
**Logic:** Trump's announcement about direct healthcare payments to Americans is political theater to attract Senate Democrats for shutdown negotiations, not a serious policy proposal  
**Reasoning:** 
* Pharmaceutical and healthcare lobbying power too strong for meaningful disruption
* Insurance serves a purpose despite being "fucked" - eliminates insurance hurts people with pre-existing conditions
* Retail traders being "more cautious this dip" creates opportunity
**Execution:** Building positions in Oscar Health (shares + calls) and considering other healthcare names  
**Risk Assessment:** If wrong about Trump follow-through, could face significant downside

### Contrarian Guidance Miss Interpretation
**Logic:** Market overreacts to minor guidance reductions, creating entry points  
**Example:** Monday.com lowered guidance by ~$2M on ~$300M revenue base - immaterial amount that doesn't justify sell-off  
**Execution:** Target entries below key psychological levels ($152 for MNDY), prefer shares when IV is elevated  
**Philosophy:** "This market punishes anybody who doesn't have exponential growth lined up" - creates opportunities when punishment is disproportionate

### Options as Leverage (Not Lottery Tickets)
**Core Principles:**
* Avoid dailies and weeklies - "never do dailies and weeklies if you can avoid it"
* Minimum 6 months to 1 year out, ideally
* At-the-money or slightly OTM strikes
* Use for leverage on conviction plays, not as "way of making a shitload of money"
* Example: JetBlue calls were 1.5 months out, ATM - went from -40% to +50%
**Risk Management:** "If you're wrong, you lose everything" with weeklies; "if you're right, you won't sell the top, you'll hold overnight and theta will destroy you"

### Precise Bottom-Calling via Catalyst Anticipation
**Method:** Identify upcoming catalysts that market isn't pricing in
**Friday Example:** Trump meeting with Hungarian Prime Minister - anticipated either:
  * Precious metals/mineral deal announcement
  * Off-the-rails economic commentary that would shift sentiment
**Timing:** Market was looking for catalyst; this was the obvious one  
**Result:** Called exact bottom, $17.50 SPY rally from that moment

### Post-Earnings Volatility Trading
**Logic:** Earnings create wild intraday swings even on good reports  
**Example:** EVGO sold off from +10% to -3% despite strong earnings (15th consecutive quarter double-digit growth, beat revenue)  
**Execution:** Tight stop losses, willingness to re-enter if stopped out and price attractive

### Long-Term Speculative "Moonshot" Positions
**Characteristics:**
* Deep out-of-the-money leaps (2027 expiry)
* Based on cultural/consumer trends research (nostalgia, "IRL loot boxes," Labubu trend)
